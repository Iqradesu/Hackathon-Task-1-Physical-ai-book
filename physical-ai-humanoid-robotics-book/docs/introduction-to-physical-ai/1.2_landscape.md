# 1.2 The Humanoid Robotics Landscape: An In-Depth Overview

## Introduction

Humanoid robots are designed to **mimic human form and behavior**.  
They are central to **physical AI research** because they combine **perception, reasoning, and action** in a human-like body.

---

## Key Features of Humanoid Robots

- **Bipedal locomotion** → walking and balancing like humans  
- **Manipulation** → hands or grippers for interacting with objects  
- **Sensors** → vision, touch, and auditory systems for perceiving the environment  
- **Cognition** → decision-making using AI models, sometimes LLM-guided

---

## Popular Humanoid Platforms

- **Boston Dynamics Atlas** → advanced mobility and agility  
- **Honda ASIMO** → smooth walking and basic manipulation  
- **NAO / Pepper** → human interaction and social AI experiments  
- **Research platforms** → flexible, programmable for VLA and AI experiments

---

## Challenges in Humanoid Robotics

- **Balance and locomotion** → keeping upright on uneven terrain  
- **Manipulation** → precise, safe object handling  
- **Perception** → integrating sensors to understand complex environments  
- **Cognitive planning** → real-time decision making for dynamic tasks

---

## Summary

Humanoid robots are **complex, versatile systems** combining physical capabilities and AI.  
Understanding their landscape is essential for **designing, testing, and deploying Physical AI systems**.
